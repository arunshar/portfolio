Arun Sharma is a Ph.D. graduate in Computer Science from the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, where he was advised by Prof. Shashi Shekhar and Prof. Vipin Kumar. His research spans Spatial AI, physics-informed deep learning, and spatiotemporal data mining, with the goal of building generative models and algorithms that embed geographic, physical, and geometric priors into deep learning for geospatial machine learning, autonomous driving, climate downscaling, and anomaly detection. He received his M.S. in Computer Science from the State University of New York at Buffalo under Prof. Varun Chandola and previously interned at Esri, where he built scalable graph-based frameworks for maritime route optimization and real-time anomaly detection. He is a recipient of the University of Minnesota Doctoral Dissertation Fellowship and has published in leading AI and spatial computing venues including NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, CVPR, AAAI, and SIGSPATIAL, where he also serves as a reviewer.
