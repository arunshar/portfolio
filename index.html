About

Physics-informed AI for reliable spatial reasoning.

I build physics-informed AI systems that make spatial reasoning reliable for real-world deployment, from detecting GPS spoofing to enable safer autonomous navigation, to modeling energy-efficient vehicle routing, to downscaling climate data for coastal flood risk assessment.

My research fuses deep generative models, diffusion models, transformers, physics-informed learning, and geostatistical priors to address data incompleteness, distribution shift, and violations of physical laws in spatial machine learning.

I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, advised by Prof. Shashi Shekhar, with committee members Prof. Vipin Kumar, Prof. Ravi Janardan, and Prof. Ying Song. At Esri, I shipped a maritime anomaly detection pipeline on AWS that improved classification accuracy from 55% to 73% and reduced API latency by 30%.

My technical work spans Python, Java, R, SQL, Scala, C/C++, Julia, Rust, Go, JavaScript, TypeScript, PyTorch, TensorFlow, JAX, XGBoost, MLflow, Hugging Face, LangChain, vector databases, PySpark, Kafka, Docker, Kubernetes, SageMaker, Lambda, ECS, Bedrock, vLLM, and SGLang.

Selected Projects

Projects

Selected projects are ordered to match the current CV. Runnable GitHub and Hugging Face demos follow as applied extensions of the same research themes.

01 / Trajectory Generation

Geo-lucid Conditional Diffusion Model

Generated realistic synthetic vehicle trajectories for autonomous driving simulation and traffic prediction by co-designing GCDM, a two-stage cascaded diffusion model that injects attributed road-network priors through map-informed latent variables, spatial cross-attention, and physics-based trajectory decomposition. It produced stronger geo-distribution similarity and velocity, acceleration, and jerk fidelity than DiffTraj, Sashimi, DiffWave, and EETG on Porto and Harbin taxi data.

02 / EV Routing

Multi-Level Bi-Objective Routing

Enabled real-time energy-vs-travel-time route planning for electric vehicles by co-designing MBOR with a boundary multigraph, Multi-level Encoded Pareto Frontier View, 2D cost-interval pruning, and multi-edge pruning. The method retrieves complete Pareto-optimal path sets over fragmented road networks and achieved more than 10x online-runtime improvement on real-world road network data.

03 / Maritime Safety

STAGD, TGARD, and DC-TGARD

Reduced manual investigation areas for illegal maritime activity such as fishing, oil transfers, and trans-shipments by up to 80% through a multi-year research program on Space-Time Aware Gap Detection. The work combines comparison-less temporal indexing, R*-tree spatial indexing, maximal-union merge over space-time prism geo-ellipses, ellipse symmetry, bi-directional pruning, and early stopping on AIS data with roughly 500K ships.

04 / Sustainable Agriculture

SM-Hybrid Surrogate Models

Accelerated expensive ecosystem simulations for sustainable agriculture with SM-Hybrid, a surrogate model for Daycent that models spatial autocorrelation and long-range tele-connections using hybrid spatial neural networks. It predicted land emissions and crop yield across Iowa, Illinois, and Indiana about 2x faster than Daycent while improving accuracy over SM-ANN baselines.

05 / Biomedical Spatial Computing

Spatially-Lucid Oncology Classification

Supported precision immunotherapy by developing domain-adapted spatially-lucid neural networks for multi-category point set classification in non-Euclidean space. The work uses spatial ensemble learning, place-calibration parameters, weighted distance learning rates, spatial domain adaptation, spatial mix-up masking, and spatial contrastive predictive coding on MxIF oncology data.

06 / Community-Engaged GIScience

COVID-19 Mobility Analysis

Built an entity-relationship model, system architecture, and implementation for analyzing aggregated mobile device data during COVID-19 policy interventions. The project delivered county-level analytics on long-duration visits to high-risk business categories and fine-resolution device count maps to epidemiologists, analysts, and policymakers in collaboration with the Minnesota Department of Management and Budget.
