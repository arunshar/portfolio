About

Research at the intersection of spatial data science, geography, and learning.

I develop methods for spatial and spatiotemporal problems where data are incomplete, conditions shift across regions, and models must respect physical constraints. My work combines spatial data science, knowledge-guided machine learning, physics-informed learning, diffusion models, transformers, and process-based scientific models.

My core research is grounded in GIScience, trajectory analysis, indoor navigation, uncertainty-aware spatial modeling, and geographic computing. The recent project demos extend that foundation into remote sensing, 3D scene reasoning, maritime anomaly detection, and spatial computing in the broader sense of digital systems reasoning about maps, movement, and environments.

I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, advised by Prof. Shashi Shekhar and Prof. Vipin Kumar. At Esri, I shipped a maritime anomaly-detection pipeline on AWS that improved classification accuracy from 55% to 73%.

I hold the University of Minnesota Doctoral Dissertation Fellowship, and my research has been published in AAAI, SIAM SDM, ACM TIST, ACM TSAS, and ACM SIGSPATIAL.
