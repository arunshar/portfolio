About this dashboard

The COVID-19 pandemic reshaped where and how long people gathered. This panel reframes the community-engaged decision-support system from Sharma et al. (AGILE: GIScience Series, 2022) as an observability dashboard. The underlying system was built with policymakers in public health, economic management, transportation, and transit to answer questions about long-duration visits to high-risk venue categories (bars, full-service restaurants, limited-service restaurants) from aggregated, privacy-protected mobile-device data, alongside fine-resolution device-count maps to expose spatial sampling bias.

The time series tracks the weekly long-duration-visit index per venue type, normalized to a pre-pandemic baseline of 100, with markers at the major Minnesota policy interventions (stay-at-home order, phased reopening, the winter 2020 dining closure, and spring 2021 reopening). The gauge and the regional panel summarize data quality and geographic spread, the two recurring concerns end-users raised when interpreting the reports.

Illustrative UI. The curves model the documented 2020 to 2021 mobility pattern and are not the project's measured weekly figures. For the measured data and the weekly report archive, see the project page.
